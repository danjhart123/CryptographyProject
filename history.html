History of Cryptography

Cryptography has been around for thousands of years with the first recorded evidence of it being found in an inscription carved around 1900 BC, in the main chamber of the tomb of the nobleman Khnumhotep II, in Egypt.

Move forward to around 100 BC to the reign of Julius Caesar and encryption is still present. Caesar would send secret messages to his armed forced using a form of encryption that is probably the most historically known, a substitution cipher known as the Caesar cipher.

Even the 1st and 2nd world wars proved that encryption was still a prominent technique of secret telling and message cracking with the development and use of Enigma machine and Morse code by the allied forces to decrypt German encrypted messages and communicate over open radio frequencies in secret.

The Enigma machine was an electro-mechanical machine invented after world war 1 that was capable of encrypting and decrypting messages which allowed up to 10^114 possible configurations meaning that it was virtually unbreakable with brute force methods.
